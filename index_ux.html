My Design Strategy

My content strategy focuses on ‘the deliverable’: ensuring the right content in the correct context makes it to the right person on time. My goal is to successfully communicate to my audience in a timely manner through appropriate and continuous engagement with the client. This brings me to my first step in understanding my audience, their motivations, and their goals.

My first step is self education. To get started on the beginning materials, I ensure I understand the content/context of the project, the primary goals, and utilize the information available to hit the ground running. I take advantage of relevant case studies that have already been conducted Some of my favorite studies have been about behavioral economics, psychological assessments, and advertising that shows buyer behavior. Depending on the goals of a project, I always take human behavior and psychology into consideration.

The next step is to take into consideration interviews with stakeholders and potential users to guide the interactive design to be both favorable for the stakeholder, and still functional for the end-user. The stakeholders are organized by the power and interest of the company. I ask questions about their motivations, objectives, and anything they are worried about. In essence “what do you want to see out of this project?”. The users are questioned about their experience, impressions, and any problems they have faced. “Was the experience functional and how did it make you feel?”

I organize a profile that highlights findings and insights from both the consumer and stakeholder perspective. Then I can more clearly define the primary and secondary audience (colleagues, friends, family, influencers, decision-makers of the primary audience). These profiles are the user personas that dive deep into what motivates and influences them. With these profiles, I can create an empathy map involving: pains, gains, how they think & feel, what they say & do, what they hear, and what they see. Along with mapping out a person, I also create a person’s journey and note their experiences. Understanding and defining the audience gives me insight on how to structure my content and reframe any problems the user may face.

My design strategy will help prioritize what is needed to improve client relationships, user experiences, and business goals. This clarity will allow for a more effective decision-making process.
